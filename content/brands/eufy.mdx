Eufy has built a genuine niche in the home security camera market by doing one thing consistently: no monthly fees. While Ring and Arlo monetise through subscriptions, Eufy stores footage locally, runs AI detection on-device, and charges you nothing after the hardware purchase.

This approach has real appeal for NZ homeowners who want a complete, functional system without an ongoing bill — but Eufy's history includes a significant privacy incident that warrants honest discussion.

## Eufy product range in NZ

### HomeBase Systems

**HomeBase 3** — Eufy's current hub device. Supports up to 16 cameras, 16GB built-in storage (expandable via USB HDD up to 16TB), local AI processing, and Wi-Fi 6 for improved camera connections. One HomeBase 3 is typically sufficient for a whole-home setup of up to 8–10 cameras.

**Eufy Cam 3** — 4K battery camera designed for HomeBase 3. Excellent outdoor performance, 4K resolution, colour night vision. Requires HomeBase 3.

**Eufy Cam 2 Pro** — 2K battery camera, solid all-round performer. Compatible with HomeBase 3 and older HomeBase 2.

**Eufy Cam 2C Pro** — More compact 2K camera with a slightly wider field of view. Good for smaller areas.

### SoloCam (Standalone)

**SoloCam S340** — Solar-powered standalone camera with built-in microSD storage (no HomeBase required). 3K resolution, dual-band Wi-Fi, 360° pan/tilt. Designed for locations with good sun exposure. No ongoing costs at all — solar powers the camera, local storage holds the footage.

**SoloCam S220** — Solar with 2K resolution and a fixed lens. Simpler and more affordable than the S340. Also requires no HomeBase.

**SoloCam E40** — Battery-powered standalone camera with 2K resolution and built-in storage (without HomeBase, storage is more limited). Good for testing a location before committing to a permanent install.

### Doorbells

**Eufy Video Doorbell Dual** — Two cameras in one doorbell: one at head height, one aimed downward for package detection. No subscription required for full functionality. Available in battery and wired versions. Stores video locally via HomeBase.

**Eufy Video Doorbell E340** — Single-lens doorbell with colour night vision and local storage. Wired power option available.

## What Eufy does well

**No ongoing costs.** Eufy's core value proposition is real. A HomeBase + cameras setup has zero required subscription. AI detection, person alerts, vehicle alerts, remote viewing — all included in the hardware purchase.

**Local storage = true privacy.** Footage stays on your HomeBase, on your network. You don't need to trust a cloud provider with your home video. This genuinely matters for many NZ homeowners, particularly after increased awareness of cloud data risks.

**HomeBase resilience.** Even if your internet goes down, Eufy cameras continue recording to the HomeBase locally. Ring and Arlo cameras typically stop recording when internet is lost (because they upload to cloud). Local NVR systems also have this resilience, but Eufy achieves it at a lower installation complexity.

**4K resolution available without subscription.** Eufy Cam 3 offers 4K video at no monthly cost. Ring's highest resolution (1080p/1536p for doorbells) is cheaper hardware, but Eufy's 4K is substantially better quality for identifying faces and licence plates (within battery camera limitations).

**SoloCam S340 is unique.** A solar-powered 3K camera with local storage and no hub requirement is genuinely unusual. For homeowners who want to add a camera to a shed, garage, or remote corner of their property without running cables, it's hard to beat.

## Eufy's honest limitations

**The 2022 privacy incident.** Eufy had documented issues with thumbnail uploads to cloud servers and unencrypted stream access — despite marketing Eufy as a cloud-free, local-only system. The company has made changes (end-to-end encryption, clearer privacy settings), but the incident is part of Eufy's track record. If privacy is your primary reason for choosing Eufy over Ring, understand this history.

**App experience.** Eufy's app (Eufy Security) is functional but consistently rated slightly below Ring and Arlo in user reviews. Navigation is occasionally counterintuitive, and some settings are buried. This has improved significantly but is still a weakness.

**Battery cameras still have wake-up delay.** Like all battery cameras, Eufy cameras have a 1–3 second wake-up delay for motion detection. This is a hardware/design constraint, not specific to Eufy. If you need evidence-grade driveway footage with no missed motion, a PoE/NVR system is more appropriate.

**Less polished ecosystem integration.** Eufy's Alexa and Google Home integration works but is less seamless than Ring/Alexa. HomeKit support exists but is limited compared to Arlo. If deep smart home integration matters to you, Ring (Alexa) or Arlo (Google/HomeKit) may suit you better.

**HomeBase is a single point of failure.** If the HomeBase is stolen, damaged, or fails, you lose access to all locally stored footage. Eufy offers cloud backup as an optional paid add-on for this reason.

## Who Eufy is right for

Eufy makes the most sense for NZ homeowners who:
- Want a complete camera system with no ongoing monthly costs
- Prefer local storage over cloud storage
- Have a 2–4 camera setup with reasonable upfront budget
- Have a property with solar-exposure for SoloCam models
- Are not deeply invested in Alexa, Google Home, or HomeKit

## Who should look elsewhere

If you're a renter without much upfront budget, Ring Stick Up Cam Battery is cheaper to start. If smart home ecosystem integration is important, Arlo (Google/HomeKit) or Ring (Alexa) are stronger. If you need evidence-grade continuous recording, a PoE/NVR system is the right choice regardless of brand.

<Callout type="tip">
**HomeBase placement tip:** Position the HomeBase 3 centrally in your home for best camera connectivity — not next to your router necessarily, but midpoint between your most distant camera positions. It uses a proprietary 2.4GHz protocol with cameras, not Wi-Fi, so line-of-sight matters.
</Callout>
